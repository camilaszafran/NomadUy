## SECTION 1 — Immigration & Legal Status

### Entry: Tourist Visa

- Most nationalities enter as tourists for **90 days**, extendable once for another 90 days (180 days total) by requesting an extension at the DNM.
- No entry fee. Uruguay is one of the most open-border countries in the region.
- Nationalities requiring a visa prior to entry: check at https://www.liveinuruguay.uy/es/entryprocedures

### Digital Nomad Permit (Permiso de Residencia para Nómadas Digitales)

- Allows remote workers to live legally in Uruguay for **6 months, renewable once** (up to 12 months total).
- After 12 months, must transition to Residencia Temporaria or Permanente.
- **Cost:** ~$312 UYU (paid via Abitab, RedPagos, or Correo Uruguayo). NOT $10 USD — this is a common misconception.
- **Requirements:** Passport, online form with personal data, sworn declaration of economic means (downloadable from DNM site). No proof of income threshold officially required, but declaration of self-sufficiency is mandatory.
- Must apply **from within Uruguay** (cannot apply remotely).
- **Apply at:** https://migracion.minterior.gub.uy/ → tramites.gub.uy
- Contact: dnm-hip@minterior.gub.uy
- Official guide (Uruguay XXI): https://www.uruguayxxi.gub.uy/es/noticias/articulo/nomades-digitales-podran-vivir-y-trabajar-legalmente-en-uruguay/

### Residencia Temporaria (Non-Mercosur)

- For people outside the Mercosur bloc who wish to stay beyond tourist status.
- Duration: **2 years**, renewable.
- **Requirements:**
  - Valid passport
  - Birth certificate (apostilled + Spanish translation)
  - Criminal background check from country of residence (apostilled + translated)
  - Medical certificate issued in Uruguay
  - Proof of income, employment, study, or property
  - 2 passport photos
- Processing time: approximately 3–6 months at DNM.
- Official tramite: https://www.gub.uy/tramites/residencia-legal-temporaria

### Residencia Temporaria Mercosur (Fast Track)

- For citizens of: **Argentina, Brasil, Paraguay, Bolivia, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Perú, Venezuela, Guyana, Surinam**.
- Significantly faster and simpler process. Duration: **2 years**, renewable once.
- **Requirements:** Identity document + criminal background check from home country.
- Brazil: no apostille required (Mercosur bilateral agreement).
- All other foreign documents must be apostilled and translated into Spanish.
- Official tramite: https://www.gub.uy/tramites/residencia-legal-temporaria-mercosur
- Contact: dnm-visasresidencias@minterior.gub.uy

### Residencia Permanente

- Available after 3 years of continuous legal residence in Uruguay (any status counts).
- Mercosur citizens: can apply for permanent residence directly, skipping temporary stage.
- Official tramite (permanent): https://www.gub.uy/tramites/residencia-legal-permanente
- Mercosur permanent: https://www.gub.uy/tramites/residencia-legal-permanente-mercosur

### Cédula de Identidad

- Uruguay's national ID card. Issued by Dirección Nacional de Identificación Civil (DNIC) once residency is approved.
- Required to open a bank account, rent an apartment, access healthcare via FONASA, get a driver's license.
- DNIC: https://www.gub.uy/ministerio-interior/dnic

### Main Immigration Office (DNM)

- **Dirección Nacional de Migración:** https://migracion.minterior.gub.uy/
- Official government tramites portal: https://www.gub.uy/tramites/residencia-legal
- Uruguay XXI (investment & residency guide): https://www.uruguayxxi.gub.uy/
- Live in Uruguay (official guide for new residents): https://www.liveinuruguay.uy/

## SECTION 2 — Taxes & Banking

### Uruguay's Territorial Tax System

Uruguay only taxes **income earned within Uruguay**. Foreign-sourced income is generally not taxed under the standard system. This is a major draw for digital nomads and investors.

**Key taxes:**
- **IRPF** (Impuesto a la Renta de las Personas Físicas): Personal income tax. Progressive scale from 0% to 36%. Applies to Uruguayan-source income only.
- **IRAE** (Impuesto a las Rentas de las Actividades Económicas): Corporate income tax, 25%. For businesses.
- **IVA**: VAT at 22% (10% reduced for basic goods).

### Tax Holiday for New Residents

New tax residents can choose one of two options for foreign-source income:
1. **10-year exemption** on foreign capital income (dividends, interest from abroad).
2. **7% flat rate permanently** on foreign capital income.

**Important change from January 1, 2026:** Capital and real estate income from non-resident entities will now be considered Uruguayan-source income for tax residents. This affects primarily investors and those with complex offshore structures. Consult a local accountant.

- **DGI (tax authority):** https://www.dgi.gub.uy/
- Guyer & Regules tax analysis: https://www.guyer.com.uy/informes-&-noticias/causales-de-la-residencia-fiscal-en-uruguay-y-opcion-de-tax-holiday

### RUT — Tax ID Number

- Required for freelancers working in Uruguay or registering a business.
- Issued by DGI. Can be obtained online or at DGI offices.
- Freelancers must register as "empresa unipersonal" to issue invoices.
- BPS (social security) registration also required for those earning Uruguayan income.
- **BPS:** https://www.bps.gub.uy/

### Banking

| Bank | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **BROU** (Banco República) | State bank. Hardest to open without cédula. Most ATMs in country. |
| **Itaú** | Good for expats. Requires cédula or residency in progress. |
| **Santander** | Similar to Itaú. Branches throughout Montevideo. |
| **BBVA** | Also accessible for expats. |
| **Prex** | App-based prepaid card. Opens with just passport or cédula. USD + UYU accounts. Very expat-friendly. |
| **OCA** | Credit card widely used, no bank account required. |

- **Wise:** Recommended for international transfers. Widely used by the expat community. Not a Uruguayan bank but essential for day-to-day.
- ATM networks: **RedBROU** and **Banred** (both accept Visa/Mastercard international cards).
- Most BROU ATMs charge no fee for international withdrawals; private bank ATMs may charge.

## SECTION 3 — Healthcare

### How the System Works

Uruguay has a dual public-private healthcare system coordinated by **SNIS** (Sistema Nacional Integrado de Salud).

- **ASSE** (Administración de los Servicios de Salud del Estado): Public health system. Free or very low cost. Longer wait times.
- **Mutualistas:** Private HMOs subsidized by the state. The main option for expats. Monthly cost ranges from $5,000–$12,000 UYU (~$100–$250 USD) depending on plan and provider.
- **FONASA** (Fondo Nacional de Salud): State fund that subsidizes mutualista costs for formal workers. Access unlocked once you have formal Uruguayan employment and contribute to BPS.

### ASSE — Public System

- Free for Uruguayan residents regardless of immigration status, with certain income conditions.
- Foreign nationals (with or without residency proceedings) can affiliate for free if they meet income criteria.
- **ASSE official:** https://www.asse.com.uy/
- Affiliation tramite: https://www.gub.uy/tramites/afiliacion-asse
- ASSE for foreigners: http://afiliaciones.asse.com.uy/prestaciones_detail?id=P-5-extranjeros

### Mutualistas (Private HMOs)

| Provider | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Médica Uruguaya** | Largest. Strong emergency service. |
| **CASMU** | Large, city-based. Good coverage. |
| **SMI** | Well-regarded for expats. |
| **Médica TCC** | Solid mid-range option. |
| **Asociación Española** | Historic, well-regarded. Age cap ~60–65 for new enrollees. |
| **British Hospital** | Bilingual staff. Age cap for new enrollees. |
| **Hospital Evangélico** | Smaller, good quality. |

- Recommended for expats: CASMU or SMI for balance of cost and service.
- FONASA comparison tool: https://atuservicio.msp.gub.uy/

### Emergencies

- **SAMU (ambulance):** 105
- **Policía:** 911
- **Bomberos:** 104
- Mutualistas have 24/7 home emergency service (servicio de emergencias a domicilio) included in plans.

## SECTION 4 — Housing & Neighborhoods

### How Renting Works

- Standard lease: 2-year contracts ("contrato de arrendamiento").
- **Garantía:** The biggest obstacle for foreigners. Options:
  1. **Garantía de propietario local:** A Uruguayan property owner who co-signs. Hardest to get as a foreigner.
  2. **ANDA** (Agencia Nacional de Vivienda): State guarantee service. Requires stable income proof.
  3. **Garantía bancaria:** Bank deposit equivalent to 12 months of rent.
  4. **Inmobiliarias sin garantía:** Some agencies specialize in foreigners — typically charge an extra month upfront.
- Coliving spaces: Best option for the first 1–3 months. No guarantee needed.
- Platforms: **Gallito** (gallito.com.uy), **Infocasas** (infocasas.com.uy), **MercadoLibre**, Facebook groups "Alquileres Montevideo extranjeros"

- **Paysandú, Rivera, Mercedes:** Budget-friendly interior cities. Good for low-budget immigrants.

## SECTION 5 — Transportation: Montevideo

### Public Bus (STM)

- **Sistema de Transporte Metropolitano (STM):** Covers Montevideo, Canelones, and San José.
- **Tarjeta STM:** Rechargeable smart card. Available at Abitab, RedPagos, STM offices. Card costs $100 UYU. Minimum recharge: $100 UYU.
- **Fares (January 2026):**
  - 1 hour (electronic): $52 UYU
  - 1 hour (cash): $64 UYU
  - 2 hours (electronic): $78 UYU
  - 2 hours (cash): $97 UYU
- **Official STM:** https://montevideo.gub.uy/area-tematica/sistema-de-transporte-metropolitano/tarjetas-stm
- **Tariffs page:** https://montevideo.gub.uy/areas-tematicas/sistema-de-transporte-metropolitano/tarifas-del-transporte-colectivo-urbano
- Main bus company: **CUTCSA** (cutcsa.com.uy). Also: COETC, COME, Raincoop.
- **App:** Cómo ir (Android/iOS) — real-time bus tracking in Montevideo.

### Rideshare & Taxis

- **Uber:** Operates legally in Uruguay. Reliable, widely used.
- **Cabify:** Also legal and well-used.
- **DiDi:** Entered Uruguay market, cheaper alternative.
- **Taxis:** Official white taxis with black stripe. App: **Easy Taxi**. Metered rides.

### Cycling

- **MiMovi:** Intendencia de Montevideo's public bike share. Docking stations throughout city.
- Extensive ciclovías (bike lanes) along the Rambla (waterfront) and main avenues.

### Car

- Driving license from any country valid for 90 days. After that, Uruguayan license or IDP required.
- Car rentals at Aeropuerto de Carrasco (Hertz, Avis, Europcar, local companies).
- Traffic: right-side driving. Generally calm outside rush hour.

## SECTION 6 — Transportation: Intercity

### Bus (Main Option)

- Uruguay has an excellent intercity bus network. Most cities reachable within 3–6 hours from Montevideo.
- **Terminal Tres Cruces:** Main Montevideo bus terminal. Gral. Flores & Bulevar Artigas.
- **URUBUS:** Aggregator platform for all intercity bus companies. Book tickets online. https://www.urubus.com.uy/
- Major bus companies: **COPSA, COT, Turil, CITA, Núñez, Bussur, EGA**.

### Carpooling — Viatik

- **Viatik:** Platform for shared car rides between cities. Not a bus — it connects drivers and passengers for long-distance trips. Focus on Montevideo–Maldonado, Salto, Paysandú routes.
- 70,000+ registered users. More flexible and often cheaper than bus.
- Article: https://www.elobservador.com.uy/ciencia-y-tecnologia/las-nuevas-apps-uruguayas-que-cambian-el-juego-la-movilidad-viatik-tripwip-y-gebo-n5943058

### Peer-to-Peer Car Rental — TripWip

- **TripWip:** Platform to rent cars from private owners. Not intercity buses. Great for road trips when you don't want to own a car.
- 400+ vehicles listed, strong growth in Uruguay.
- Partnered with Viatik: TripWip renters can post their route on Viatik.

### Ferry (Montevideo ↔ Buenos Aires / Colonia)

- **Buquebus:** Montevideo ↔ Buenos Aires (port to port). ~3 hours. https://www.buquebus.com/
- **Seacat / FRS:** Colonia ↔ Buenos Aires. ~1 hour. Faster and cheaper if you're near Colonia.
- Very common for expats visiting Argentina or Argentines visiting Uruguay.

### Flights

- **Aeropuerto Internacional de Carrasco (MVD):** Montevideo. Main international airport.
- **Aeropuerto de Punta del Este (PDP):** Seasonal flights, mainly Dec–Feb.
- Domestic flights limited. PLUNA no longer exists; some charter services.
- LATAM, Aerolíneas Argentinas, American, Air France, Iberia, Copa main carriers.

## SECTION 8 — Work & Remote Work

### Digital Nomads: Legal Status

- Working remotely for foreign clients/employers while in Uruguay is legal.
- The Digital Nomad Permit (Section 1) regularizes this legally.
- No Uruguayan taxes on foreign-source income for the first 10 years (tax holiday).

### Freelancers Earning Uruguayan Income

- Must register with **DGI** (Dirección General Impositiva) and obtain a **RUT** (tax ID).
- Must register with **BPS** (social security) and pay monthly contributions.
- Can operate as "empresa unipersonal" (sole proprietorship) to issue invoices to Uruguayan clients.
- **DGI:** https://www.dgi.gub.uy/
- **BPS:** https://www.bps.gub.uy/

### Coworking Spaces (Montevideo)

| Space | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Sinergia** | Pocitos, Ciudad Vieja, Tres Cruces | Largest network, premium |
| **Crea Cowork** | Cordón | Popular with startups |
| **WeWork** | WTC Montevideo | International standard |
| **La Maquinista** | Prado | Creative community |
| **Modum** | Pocitos | Good WiFi, quiet |

- Internet: Uruguay has excellent fiber infrastructure via **ANTEL** (state telecom). Speeds of 100–500 Mbps widely available. Ranked among best in South America.
- **ANTEL:** https://www.antel.com.uy/

## SECTION 9 — Tourism: Montevideo

### Must-See

- **Rambla de Montevideo:** 22km waterfront promenade. Central to city life. Walking, cycling, sunset watching.
- **Ciudad Vieja:** Colonial old town. Plaza Independencia, Teatro Solís, Mercado del Puerto (traditional grilled meat market). Best on Saturday midday.
- **Mercado Agrícola de Montevideo (MAM):** Food hall. Palermo/Goes area. Artisan food, restaurants, market.
- **Palermo & Parque Rodó:** Arts, street food, galleries, lake.
- **Cerro de Montevideo:** Hill fortress with panoramic views of the bay.
- **Barrio Sur / Cordón:** Murga culture, tango, candombe (Uruguayan African drumming tradition).

### Events & Culture

- **Carnaval de Montevideo:** February. Longest carnival in the world (40+ days). Candombe parades, murga theater, llamadas.
- **Lollapalooza Uruguay:** Annual music festival, Parque Batlle.
- **Mercado de los Artesanos:** Crafts market, Ciudad Vieja. Permanent.
- **Feria de Tristán Narvaja:** Sunday flea market, Cordón. Classic Montevideo experience.

## SECTION 10 — Tourism: Whole Country

### Colonia del Sacramento (Colonia dept.)

- **2.5 hours from Montevideo** by bus, 1 hour by ferry from Buenos Aires.
- UNESCO World Heritage Site. Portuguese-Spanish colonial town.
- Must-do: Barrio Histórico cobblestone streets, Faro (lighthouse), Portón de Campo ruins, waterfront.
- Excellent for a weekend trip or day trip.
- **Official tourism:** https://asociacionturisticacolonia.com.uy/
- Ferry from BA: Buquebus or Seacat.

### Maldonado / Punta del Este

- Uruguay's international resort city. Peak season December–February.
- **Casapueblo** (Carlos Páez Vilaró): Artist's home/museum, Punta Ballena. Iconic for sunset.
- **La Mano en la Arena** (Mario Irarrázabal sculpture on Playa Brava). Uruguay's most photographed spot.
- **La Brava / La Mansa:** Two sides of the peninsula — Atlantic surf vs. calmer River Plate.
- **Piriápolis:** Nearby, quieter, more affordable, good for families.
- **Punta Ballena:** Spectacular views, eco-resort area.
- Very expensive Dec–Feb. Off-season (March–November): peaceful and affordable.

### Rocha — Official: https://turismorocha.gub.uy/

Uruguay's wildest, most preserved coastal department.

- **Cabo Polonio:** Remote lighthouse village. No roads, no grid electricity. Accessed by 4x4 trucks from Route 10. Sea lions colony on the rocks. Protected area managed by SNAP. Dunes, silence, stars. Peak season Dec–Feb, possible year-round. Must-book trucks in advance. Wildlife/conservation: https://vidasilvestre.org.uy
- **Punta del Diablo:** Fishing village turned bohemian/backpacker destination. Rugged beaches, cabins, casual restaurants.
- **La Paloma:** Main Rocha beach town. Sheltered harbor, good for families. Sailing community.
- **La Pedrera:** Small, surfer and bohemian vibe. Boutique and upscale options.
- **Parque Nacional Santa Teresa:** Fortress ruins, beaches, pine forest, free camping. One of Uruguay's best nature parks.
- **Laguna de Rocha, Laguna Garzón:** Birdwatching, kiteboarding, fishing.

### Lavalleja — Official: https://lavalleja.uy/

- **Minas:** Capital city. Gateway to the Sierras del Este. Charming, very Uruguayan, affordable.
- **Cerro Arequita:** Rocky hill with caves. Geological wonder. Guided visits, restaurant at base.
- **Grutas del Palacio (Geoparque):** UNESCO-aspiring geopark. Columnar basalt formations. One of Uruguay's most unique geological sites. https://www.geoparque.uy/
- **Manantiales Serranos:** Hot spring resort + nature. Camping, trails, relaxation. https://manantialesserranos.uy/
- **Villa Serrana:** Tiny mountain village. Architect Julio Vilamajó's summer town. Hiking trails, streams. Very remote and peaceful.
- Only 1.5h from Montevideo — excellent for weekend trips.

### Canelones — Official: https://atccanelones.com.uy/

- Wine country. Uruguay's signature grape: **Tannat**.
- Close to Montevideo (30–60 min). Easy day trip.
- Key wineries: **Bodega Bouza**, **Pisano**, **Carrau**, **Juanicó**, **De Lucca**, **Alto de la Ballena**.
- Wine routes, tastings, lunches. Growing agrotourism circuit.
- Also: beaches (Atlántida, Piriápolis border), El Pinar, La Floresta.

### Litoral — Salto & Paysandú

- **Destino Termas official:** https://destinotermas.gub.uy/
- **Salto official tourism:** https://turismo.salto.gub.uy/

**Salto (5–6h from Montevideo):**
- Uruguay's second-largest city.
- **Termas del Daymán:** 6km from Salto. 11+ thermal pools, water parks, hotels. Open year-round. Peak: May–August (winter).
- **Termas de Salto Grande:** 13km from Salto. Scenic, natural setting on Salto Grande reservoir.
- **Salto Grande Dam:** Massive binational hydroelectric dam with Argentina. Notable architecture and views.
- **Turismo Salto:** https://turismo.salto.gub.uy/termal

**Paysandú (4.5h from Montevideo):**
- Termas de Almirón, Termas de Guaviyú (60km north, more natural setting).
- Strong Carnival tradition (February).
- Gateway to Litoral Argentina via international bridge.
- **Destino Binacional** (Paysandú–Colón, Argentina cross-border tourism): https://www.destinobinacional.uy

### Tacuarembó — Official: https://www.tacuaremboturismo.gub.uy/

- Deep gaucho culture heartland. Authentic Uruguayan countryside experience.
- **Valle del Lunarejo:** One of Uruguay's most dramatic landscapes. Deep ravine with subtropical vegetation, birds, streams, silence. Very remote (good road from Tacuarembó). https://www.lunarejo.uy
- **Festival de la Patria Gaucha:** March, in Tacuarembó. Largest gaucho festival in the region. Horseback riding, traditional crafts, music. Massive.
- Connection to **Carlos Gardel** (claimed birthplace, though disputed with Argentina).
- Arequita region, camping, birdwatching.

### Rivera

- Northern border city with Brazil (Santana do Livramento across the street).
- Unique bilingual border culture. Mixed food, architecture, and commerce.
- Duty-free shops.
- Much cooler climate in winter.

### Cerro Largo

- **Melo:** Capital. Quiet, authentic interior Uruguay.
- Sierras de Melo, Quebrada de los Cuervos (stunning gorge with subtropical vegetation, birdwatching). National Park.

### Florida, Flores, Durazno, Treinta y Tres

- Less-visited interior departments.
- **Quebrada de los Cuervos** (Treinta y Tres): Spectacular gorge. Protected area.
- **Valle Edén** (Tacuarembó): Forest, streams, palms. Off-grid eco-tourism.
- **Paso Centurión** (Cerro Largo): Remote ecotourism, birdwatching hotspot.

## SECTION 11 — Trekking, Trails & Outdoor

- **Club de Trekking y Senderismo del Uruguay:** https://www.facebook.com/clubtrekkinguy/

### Trail Databases

## SECTION 12 — Community & Culture

### Practical Culture Notes for Newcomers

- **Mate:** Uruguay has the highest mate consumption per capita in the world. It is not just a drink — carrying a thermos and sharing mate is a daily social ritual. Refusing mate is occasionally seen as unfriendly.
- **Asado:** The social backbone of Uruguayan life. Expected to happen at least weekly in any community. Learn the basics: parrillero (grill master), leña (wood) vs. carbón (charcoal), punto justo (right cook point).
- **Pace:** Uruguay is calmer than Argentina or Brazil. Business moves slower. Bureaucracy especially. Budget extra time for everything.
- **Language:** Spanish (Rioplatense, similar to Buenos Aires accent, with voseo). In Rivera and border areas, mix of Spanish + Portuguese ("Portuñol"). Most younger professionals speak some English. Very few services in English outside tourism.
- **Tipping:** 10% at restaurants is standard. Not mandatory but expected. Uber: no tip expected but appreciated.
- **Safety:** One of the safest countries in Latin America. Normal urban precautions apply in Tres Cruces, Ciudad Vieja, Casavalle areas at night.
- **Currency:** UYU (peso uruguayo). USD widely accepted in real estate, tourist areas. ATMs dispense UYU. Most transactions in UYU.

## Research Notes & Caveats

- **Prices:** All UYU prices subject to inflation (~8–10%/year). USD-denominated prices more stable. Always note "verify current price" when publishing.
- **Digital Nomad Permit cost:** ~$312 UYU as of 2024–2025 (NOT $10 USD — that figure is outdated/incorrect).
- **Tax Holiday:** Changed January 2026. Capital income from non-resident entities is now taxed for fiscal residents. Affects investors more than typical digital nomads.
- **Viatik ≠ bus:** It is a carpooling platform, not an intercity bus service. TripWip = P2P car rental.
- **Mercosur residency:** Citizens of listed countries have a significantly faster, simpler path to residency. Always recommend this route to eligible readers.
- **Healthcare costs:** Mutualista costs often quoted in UYU. $5,000–12,000 UYU/month range = ~$100–250 USD. This varies significantly by provider and age. Consult atuservicio.msp.gub.uy for current quotes.
